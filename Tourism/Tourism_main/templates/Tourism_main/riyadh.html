Al Masmak Palace

The construction of Al-Masmak Palace dates back to the year 1895 during the reign of Muhammad bin Abdullah bin Rashid at the hands of builders who were keen to create it in a style similar to the buildings of the Emirate of Rashid. The palace is considered to have an important historical status in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia and one of the national monuments due to it being a battlefield in which King Abdulaziz Al Saud of the city of Riyadh after a great epic against the Rashid family in 1902. The palace was used from then on as a fortress to store ammunition and weapons. It was then transformed into a distinguished archaeological museum that was opened in 1995 AD under the supervision of Prince Salman bin Abdulaziz after it was prepared and restored by the Riyadh Municipality. With the participation of the Ministry of Education and the Higher Authority for Development,the reason for calling Al-Masmak Palace this name is due to its old name, which is Al-Masmak Palace, which was called due to the thickness of its walls and multiple floors.

Although it is considered an archaeological and historical place; However, you can visit it and do some recreational activities in addition to those that help you enrich the historical culture.

Saudi National Museum

From the Earth to the moon, and from prehistoric times to the modern era, the corridors of the Saudi National Museum take you on an exciting journey, transcending time and space, and standing on artistic and historical evidence in the heart of the city of Riyadh in the Al-Murabba’ district, next to King Abdulaziz Palace, where the museum opened in the year 1419 AH, on an area of 17 thousand square meters, in which the stories of 3,700 heritage pieces are spread, including statues, manuscripts, and unparalleled sculptures.

"Historic Murabba Palace" A window into Riyadh's past, and a scene from its ancient memory, is embodied in the walls of the historic Murabba Palace, which covers an area of 1,680 square meters, as it overflows with the memory of the place, the historical events that it witnessed, and the glory that was created on its land.

Authentic Urban Heritage

During your visit to the historic Murabba Palace, you will notice the distinctive design of the inner courtyard in the middle of the palace, overlooked by all rooms. It was built in the prevailing manner at that time, conveying to us a unique architectural art. The surrounding environment left its mark on the components of the palace, as the rooms and their ceilings were covered with tamarisk trunks and decorated with palm branches and fronds. The palace’s bases were made of stone, and its walls were made of mud bricks mixed with straw. They were also decorated with engravings and decorations printed on plaster. Despite the simplicity of the building's features, its beauty was evident in the winding columns that support the palace's rooms and corridors.

The palace is characterized by a square-shaped building surrounded by walls, in a depiction and embodiment of the nature of the local community that maintains the privacy of homes. It contains 32 rooms distributed over two floors, in addition to reception halls for the king’s guests, and administrative offices for his aides and guards. Enjoy discovering what the rooms contain on a historical tour inside the palace, including collectibles, clothing, and souvenir photos of the king with his children and with a number of country leaders. You can enjoy a trip in which you will experience the past of Riyadh.
