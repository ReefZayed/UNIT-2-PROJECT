Explore AlUla

OLD TOWN ,Stroll among the ruins of mudbrick buildings dating back almost a millennium

The ancient city's landmarks tell the story of its human civilization that flourished on ancient trade routes. The old town, part of the old town of AlUla, which was named the best tourist village by the World Tourism Organization, includes a maze of archaeological alleys with hundreds of mud-brick houses and a historic castle guarding its wall. The ancient people of Al-Ula built their city from mud houses lined up next to each other like an impenetrable fortress, to defend themselves and protect their city from external dangers. In the past, the city wall included 14 gates that the people of Al-Ula opened in the morning and closed in the evening daily over the years. Researchers and historians were able to collect oral memories and details of daily life within the town walls, and these stories will be available to introduce visitors to the old town of AlUla to the living memory of this exceptional destination.

THE OASIS AlUla's Ancient Agricultural Heartland

The Oasis is part of the AlUla Old Town Village, awarded title of Best Tourism Village by the United Nations World Tourism Organization (UNWTO). Ancient civilisations have cultivated this spring-fed oasis for millennia, and today it remains a lush and fertile plain with sustainable farming at its core. Idyllic walking trails meander though picturesque scenery too, while a collection of cafes and restaurants offer a wealth of welcoming spots for rest, relaxation and contemplation.

JABAL IKMAH , Millennia Etched in Stone

Considered one of the archaeological sites in Al-Ula. It includes thousands of writings and inscriptions dating back to distant times, preceding the emergence of the Arabic language that we know today. However, the evidence found in Jabal Ikma has an exceptional historical status. UNESCO has officially included Mount Ikma on the “Memory of the World” register to emphasize the importance of the writings and inscriptions engraved on its rocks thousands of years ago. We invite archeology lovers to visit this exceptional site, dating back to around the first millennium BC, and dive into the world of the past. The rock-carved art depicts human faces, musical instruments, and animals to tell the stories of the Dedanite and Lihyanite civilizations that settled in the land of Al-Ula.

MARAYA Reflecting AlUla’s Beauty, the World’s Largest Mirrored Building

Meaning ‘mirror’ or ‘reflection’ in Arabic, Maraya is a stunning piece of art set in the desert canyon of Ashar Valley. Designed to blend into the surrounding landscape, it rises from the sand like a mirage. The state-of-the-art structure is covered in 9,740 mirrored panels reflecting AlUla's beauty and, according to Guinness World Records, making it the largest mirrored building in the world. Maraya also celebrates AlUla's significant role in history as a crossroads of cultures for centuries. The mirrors themselves become a contemporary canvas, reflecting the area’s remarkable heritage.
